ABOUT

An international forum for high school student research

ASCENT Student Research Forum 2026 is the second edition of the Online International Research Conference for High School Students in Asia, which was successfully held for the first time last year. Starting this year, the forum will be held under the shorter name “ASCENT Student Research Forum.”

The forum is organized by Chiba University STELLA Program ASCENT-6E in collaboration with partner universities in Asia. It aims to foster the next generation of international talent in science and technology by providing high school students with opportunities to present their research in English, exchange ideas with peers, and explore how young research can contribute to society.

WORKSHOP SESSION

Connecting Our Research with Society

2026 Focus: Research in the Age of AI: Opportunities and Responsibilities

Based on the research presented in the morning session, students will discuss how their research connects with society and the SDGs. They will also explore possible roles of AI in their research fields and consider the responsibilities and risks associated with its use.

What social issue does your research address?
Which SDGs are most closely related to your research?
Could AI support the development, application or communication of your research?
What risks or responsibilities should be considered when using AI?

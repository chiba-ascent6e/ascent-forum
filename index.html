ABOUT

An international forum for high school student research

This online international forum brings together high school students from across Asia to present their research in English, exchange ideas with their peers, and explore how young research can contribute to society.

The conference is hosted by Chiba University STELLA Program ASCENT-6E in collaboration with partner universities in Asia.

Students will have opportunities to:

  • Present their research in an international setting
  • Engage in discussion with peers from other countries
  • Consider links between their research, society and the SDGs
  • Build an international network through shared inquiry

PRESENTATION SESSION

Research Presentation Session

Students will share their research in English and receive questions and comments from chairpersons, session assistants and peers.

TIME

09:00–12:30 JST

The session will be conducted online via Zoom breakout rooms.

FORMAT

Oral Presentation

Students should use PowerPoint or Google Slides and deliver their presentation in English.

TIME ALLOCATION

15 minutes per presentation

10-minute oral presentation followed by 5-minute Q&A.

FIELDS

All research fields welcome

Natural Sciences, Applied Sciences and Social Sciences, including Chemistry, Biology, Geology, Physics, Mathematics and related fields.

Important Notes

  • The first slide should include the research title, student name(s), school name and grade.
  • Both individual and group research projects are welcome.
  • For group presentations, the same time allocation applies regardless of the number of presenters.
  • Please select and rank the three SDGs that are most closely related to your research.

WORKSHOP SESSION

Connecting Our Research with Society

2026 Focus: Research in the Age of AI: Opportunities and Responsibilities

Based on the research presented in the morning session, students will discuss how their research connects with society and the SDGs. They will also explore possible roles of AI in their research fields and consider the responsibilities and risks associated with its use.

What social issue does your research address?
Which SDGs are most closely related to your research?
Could AI support the development, application or communication of your research?
What risks or responsibilities should be considered when using AI?
